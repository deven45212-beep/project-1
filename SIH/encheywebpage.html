Enchey Monastery

The Solitary Temple with the Flying Lama.

The Solitary Temple

Nestled in the upper reaches of Gangtok, the vibrant capital of Sikkim, Enchey Monastery holds a unique and revered place in the state's spiritual landscape. Its name translates to "the solitary temple," a fitting description for the hermitage founded here by the great Nyingma master Lama Druptob Karpo. Renowned for his supposed ability to fly, Lama Druptob Karpo chose this serene location for his meditation, sanctifying the ground with his presence. The monastery itself was established in 1909 by the 10th Chogyal, Sikyong Tulku, and was designed in the style of a Chinese pagoda, giving it a distinctive architectural flair.

Belonging to the Nyingma order of Tibetan Buddhism, Enchey is believed to be under the special protection of the deities Khangchendzonga and Yabdean. For the residents of Gangtok, it is a primary place of worship, and it is a local custom for newlyweds to seek blessings here. The monastery is most famous for its vibrant Chaam dances, performed during the Pang Lhabsol festival, which celebrates the consecration of Mount Khangchendzonga as the guardian deity of Sikkim. With its rich legends, unique architecture, and deep connection to the local community, Enchey Monastery offers a profound glimpse into the living heart of Sikkimese Buddhism.

Kartok Monastery

A pillar of faith in the first capital of Sikkim, Yuksom.

The Seat of Lama Kartok Kunto Zangpo

Located in the historic village of Yuksom, the first capital of Sikkim, Kartok Monastery holds a place of profound importance in the state's origin story. It is one of the three seminal monasteries founded to commemorate the meeting of the three great lamas—Lhatsun Chempo, Sempa Chempo, and Rigdzin Chempo—who consecrated Phuntsog Namgyal as the first Chogyal (Dharma King) of Sikkim in 1642. This monastery is specifically associated with Lama Kartok Kunto Zangpo, one of these revered figures who journeyed from Tibet to establish the Buddhist kingdom. Its serene location, surrounded by lush forests and overlooking the ancient coronation throne of Norbugang, connects it directly to the very roots of Sikkimese identity and faith.

While perhaps more modest in scale compared to Rumtek or Pemayangtse, Kartok Monastery exudes an atmosphere of deep authenticity and tranquil spirituality. The current structure, painted in a beautiful lake-red color, is a newer building that replaced the original, older gompa. Inside, it is adorned with exquisite murals, thangkas, and statues, preserving the rich iconography of the Nyingma school of Tibetan Buddhism. The monastery is a vital center for the local community, upholding ancient rituals and teachings. For visitors, a trip to Kartok is more than a tour; it is a step back in time to the very foundation of Sikkim, offering a peaceful and contemplative space to appreciate the kingdom's unique heritage.

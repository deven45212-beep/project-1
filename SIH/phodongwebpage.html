Phodong Monastery

The heart of the Kagyu lineage in Sikkim.

The Legacy of the Karmapas

Perched on a gentle slope in North Sikkim, Phodong Monastery is one of the six major monasteries in the state and a vital center for the Kagyu school of Tibetan Buddhism. Originally founded in the early 18th century by the 4th Chogyal of Sikkim, Gyurmed Namgyal, it was built to serve as a principal seat of the Karma Kagyu lineage, which traces its spiritual authority back to the Karmapas. The monastery's religious significance is profound, as it houses the sacred relics and artifacts of this esteemed lineage and has long been guided by the spiritual presence of its successive abbots.

Today, Phodong stands as a vibrant testament to this rich heritage. The monastery was beautifully rebuilt in the 20th century and is renowned for its exquisite and well-preserved murals, thangkas, and frescoes that adorn its inner walls, depicting scenes from the lives of Buddhist saints and deities with remarkable detail and color. It is home to a community of around 260 monks who dedicate their lives to study and ritual. The monastery comes alive during its annual festival, held on the 28th and 29th days of the tenth Tibetan month, where the famous Chaam dances are performed. For visitors, Phodong offers not only architectural beauty and artistic treasures but also a living, breathing connection to the spiritual heart of Sikkim.

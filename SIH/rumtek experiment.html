Welcome to Rumtek

Discover the heart of Tibetan Buddhism in Sikkim.

The Dharmachakra Centre

Perched atop a hill facing the city of Gangtok, Rumtek Monastery is a beacon of Tibetan Buddhism and one of the most significant monastic institutions in the world. Originally built in the mid-18th century, it was re-established in the 1960s by the 16th Karmapa, Rangjung Rigpe Dorje, who fled Tibet. The monastery, also known as the Dharmachakra Centre, was designed to be a replica of the Tsurphu Monastery in Tibet, serving as the main seat of the Karma Kagyu lineage in exile. Its architecture is a breathtaking spectacle of traditional Tibetan design, characterized by vibrant murals, intricate woodcarvings, and a majestic golden stupa containing the relics of the 16th Karmapa.

The monastery complex is a sprawling center for Buddhist studies and spiritual practice. It houses a magnificent prayer hall filled with larger-than-life statues of Buddhist deities, ancient thangkas (silk paintings), and invaluable religious scriptures. The atmosphere within is one of profound tranquility and reverence, where the air hums with the gentle chanting of monks and the fluttering of prayer flags carrying blessings on the wind. Beyond its spiritual significance, Rumtek is a cultural treasure, preserving the rich artistic and philosophical heritage of Tibetan Buddhism for future generations and offering a peaceful sanctuary for seekers from all corners of the globe.

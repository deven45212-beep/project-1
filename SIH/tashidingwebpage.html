Tashiding Monastery

Where a glimpse is said to wash away all sins.

The Hill of the Auspicious Fortune

Considered the spiritual heart of Sikkim, Tashiding Monastery sits majestically on a conical hill overlooking the confluence of the sacred Rathong Chu and Rangeet rivers. Its name, meaning "The Devoted Central Glory," reflects its paramount importance in Tibetan Buddhism. Legend holds that the great master Guru Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche) shot an arrow into the air, vowing to meditate where it landed; the arrow fell on this very hill, consecrating the land in the 8th century. The monastery itself was founded in the 17th century by Ngadak Sempa Chempo, one of the three lamas who performed the consecration of the first Chogyal (King) of Sikkim. Because of this profound history, Tashiding is considered the most sacred and holy monastery in the region.

The monastery is most renowned for the Thongwa Rangdol Chorten, a sacred stupa believed to cleanse the sins of anyone who merely lays eyes upon it. This belief draws thousands of pilgrims, especially during the Bhumchu festival. Held annually on the 14th and 15th days of the first Tibetan month, this unique ceremony involves the opening of a sacred pot of holy water, the level of which is said to foretell the fortunes of the coming year. The monastery complex, dotted with numerous chortens, mani walls (stone plates inscribed with mantras), and fluttering prayer flags, exudes an aura of deep and abiding peace. For pilgrims and travelers alike, Tashiding is not just a destination but a powerful spiritual experience.
